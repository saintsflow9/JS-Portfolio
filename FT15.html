Field Trips of 2015

This was the first real field experience that would test us.

Quick promo of the first two field trips of 2015

Glens of Tekoa

Mapping

This field trip introduced us to field mapping, rock classification and the harsh weather. The stormy weather was so intense that my phone was water logged despite it being in my backpack. My data was erased and only a few photos survived.
Students were required to interpret field geology based on observations recorded. The fundamental skills that are developed are mapping, cross-sections, stratigraphic columns and a report writing on the geological history.

Topographic and geological map of the geology in Glens of Tekoa


West Coast Geological Groups

Field Techniques

The second field trip of 2015 took us to many different locations on the Southern West Coast of New Zealand. This field trip has built on all the skills from the previous field trip: mapping, sketching, observation, interpretations, note taking, stratigraphic columns and report writing.

The many sites around the West Coast region brought us to many types of outcrops, sedimentary, igneous and metamorphic. The assignments remained consistent with mapping, stratigraphic columns, observations and report writing.
